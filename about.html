The Origin of the Genre

The term "Souls-like" refers to games that follow the design principles established by FromSoftware's Demon's Souls and Dark Souls series. These games have created a subgenre known for their challenging gameplay, methodical combat, and atmospheric worlds.

Core Gameplay Elements

  • ✓ High difficulty with punishment for mistakes
  • ✓ Checkpoint system (bonfires in Dark Souls)
  • ✓ Retrieval of lost resources upon death
  • ✓ Environmental storytelling
  • ✓ Interconnected world design
  • ✓ Tactical, stamina-based combat

Evolution of the Genre

From its beginnings with Demon's Souls in 2009, the genre has expanded to include various interpretations across different styles including 2D platformers, metroidvanias, and even anime-inspired titles. The success of Elden Ring in 2022 demonstrated the mainstream appeal of these challenging games.

What Makes a Game "Souls-like"?

Souls-like games are characterized by their uncompromising difficulty, deep lore, and rewarding gameplay loop. Players are expected to learn from their mistakes and overcome challenges through perseverance and skill.

Community and Culture

The Souls-like community is known for its dedication and helpfulness. From message systems that allow players to leave tips for one another to cooperative multiplayer features, these games foster a unique sense of camaraderie among players facing similar challenges.
