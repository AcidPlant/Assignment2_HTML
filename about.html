The Origin of the Genre

The term "Souls-like" refers to games that follow the design principles established by FromSoftware's Demon's Souls and Dark Souls series. These games have created a subgenre known for their challenging gameplay, methodical combat, and atmospheric worlds.

Core Gameplay Elements

  • ✔ High difficulty with punishment for mistakes
  • ✔ Checkpoint system (bonfires in Dark Souls)
  • ✔ Retrieval of lost resources upon death
  • ✔ Environmental storytelling
  • ✔ Interconnected world design
  • ✔ Tactical, stamina-based combat

Frequently Asked Questions

What exactly makes a game "Souls-like"?

+

Souls-like games are characterized by several key features: challenging difficulty that requires patience and skill, a stamina-based combat system that punishes button mashing, the loss of currency/experience upon death with one chance to retrieve it, interconnected world design with shortcuts that loop back, minimal hand-holding with environmental storytelling, and boss battles that require pattern recognition and precise timing.

Which game should I start with as a beginner?

+

For beginners, we recommend starting with either Dark Souls Remastered for the classic experience, or Elden Ring for a more modern and accessible entry point. Elden Ring offers more flexibility with its open world design, allowing you to explore elsewhere if you're stuck. Alternatively, Code Vein provides an anime-styled souls-like with slightly more forgiving mechanics and AI companions to help you.

Are all Souls-like games extremely difficult?

+

While difficulty is a hallmark of the genre, not all souls-likes are equally punishing. Games like Code Vein and Mortal Shell offer more accessible experiences, while titles like Sekiro and Nioh 2 are considered among the most challenging. Many modern souls-likes also include difficulty options or assist modes. The key is that these games reward patience, observation, and skill improvement rather than raw reflexes alone.

What's the difference between Souls-like and Soulsborne?

+

"Soulsborne" specifically refers to FromSoftware's games: the Dark Souls series, Bloodborne, Sekiro, and Elden Ring. "Souls-like" is the broader term for any game that follows the design philosophy established by these titles, including games from other developers like Hollow Knight, Salt and Sanctuary, and Lies of P. All Soulsborne games are souls-likes, but not all souls-likes are Soulsborne games.

Do I need to play these games in any particular order?

+

Most souls-like games are standalone experiences with their own worlds and stories. While the Dark Souls trilogy shares a connected universe, you can start with any game. Each title tells its story through environmental details and item descriptions rather than traditional cutscenes, so you won't be lost jumping into any entry. However, playing Dark Souls 1 before 3 can enhance your appreciation of certain callbacks and references.

What's the appeal of such challenging games?

+

The appeal lies in the sense of genuine accomplishment. Unlike many modern games that guide players extensively, souls-likes respect player intelligence and skill. Every victory feels earned, not given. The games create memorable moments through challenge - defeating a tough boss after multiple attempts creates a rush that easier games can't replicate. Additionally, the mysterious storytelling, atmospheric worlds, and tight gameplay mechanics create an immersive experience that rewards dedication.

Are there any 2D souls-like games?

+

Yes! The souls-like formula translates excellently to 2D. Notable examples include Hollow Knight (a metroidvania with souls-like difficulty and mechanics), Salt and Sanctuary (essentially 2D Dark Souls), Blasphemous (gothic religious horror), and Dead Cells (roguelike souls-like). These games maintain the challenging combat, atmospheric storytelling, and death mechanics while adding platforming elements.

Can I play souls-like games cooperatively?

+

Many souls-like games offer cooperative multiplayer features. Dark Souls, Elden Ring, and Code Vein allow you to summon other players or NPCs to help with challenging areas and bosses. However, this usually comes with trade-offs like increased boss health or invasion risks. Some games like Salt and Sanctuary and Outward offer full co-op campaigns. The co-op features vary by game, so check each title's specific multiplayer options.

Community and Culture

The Souls-like community is known for its dedication and helpfulness. From message systems that allow players to leave tips for one another to cooperative multiplayer features, these games foster a unique sense of camaraderie among players facing similar challenges.

The Philosophy of Challenge

Souls-like games represent a philosophy: that overcoming genuine difficulty creates more meaningful experiences than effortless victory. They trust players to learn, adapt, and improve rather than lowering the bar. This respect for player capability has resonated with millions worldwide.
